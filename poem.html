For My Bestfriend
It all began back in sixth grade,
Two souls who somehow collided, stayed.
Zarmeen, the girl who turned my dull world bright,
Who made even silence feel light.
I still remember that day you called me your best friend,
And something inside me just… changed.
I didn’t even know a boy and girl could be that close,
But you, you broke that thought like it was nothing.
We laughed, we shared, we lived through tiny stories
You knew my secrets before I did.
And for me, it wasn’t friendship anymore,
It was peace. It was home.
But then came the mess, the storm, the silence.
Some misunderstanding, some stupid influence,
And suddenly, we weren’t us anymore.
The ones who couldn’t shut up turned into strangers.
You came back, Zarmeen. You said sorry.
You cried. You tried.
But I was too cruel, too frozen, too full of ego.
You dropped your pride; I held mine tighter.
And every time you cried, I looked away.
Now when I remember, it burns
I want to go back and slap that version of me,
Scream at him for breaking something so pure.
I treated a princess like she was nothing,
And it still haunts me every damn day.
Days turned to months, and months to a year.
And one day I missed you, Zarmeen.
No reason, no trigger just an ache.
And when I finally reached out,
You didn’t question, you didn’t accuse.
You just… forgave.
Without a sorry, without an explanation.
You let me back in like nothing happened,
And that’s when I realized
You weren’t like anyone else.
When everyone left, when fake friends turned their backs,
You were there, still explaining, still calming,
Still being my Zaruu, the one who never gave up on me.
But life doesn’t stop testing, does it?
The day you said, “You’re my second best friend now,”
Something inside me broke so loud
I swear the walls heard it.
“Second”. Just one word
But it stabbed like a truth I deserved.
I smiled outside, but inside I wanted to scream,
“How did I lose you, Zaruu? How?”
You gave me everything care, loyalty, love
And I gave you pain and silence.
That day, I wanted to fall on my knees,
To touch your feet, to say sorry till my voice broke.
I would’ve done anything, anything,
For one more chance to be your first again.
Zarmeen, you’re not just my best friend.
You’re the mirror that showed me who I am.
You’re the calm after chaos,
The teacher life sent wrapped as a blessing.
You’re more mature, more kind, more strong
Than I could ever hope to be.
You held my broken pieces like they mattered,
Even when I was the one who dropped yours.
You never reminded me of my mistakes
You just showed me what forgiveness looks like.
You’re my punishment and my peace.
My regret and my redemption.
And I don’t deserve you but I’m still thankful you exist.
So here’s what I swear, Zarmeen
I’ll never let my pride speak louder than my heart again.
I’ll protect this bond like it’s sacred,
Because it is. You are.
I’ll earn back every bit of trust I broke,
Even if it takes forever.
You’re not my “best friend” anymore
You’re my constant, my reminder, my lesson.
And if God ever asked me what I want back in life,
I’d say one thing
“Give me Zaruu again… and this time, I won’t let her go.”
